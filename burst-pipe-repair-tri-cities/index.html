Fast Burst Pipe Repair Across the Tri-Cities

A burst pipe can release hundreds of gallons of water per hour, causing thousands of dollars in damage to your home. Quick response is critical. Our emergency plumbers respond rapidly across Coquitlam, Port Moody, and Port Coquitlam to stop the damage and repair the pipe.

We handle all types of pipe failures — copper, PEX, PVC, ABS, and galvanized steel. Many older Tri-Cities homes still have galvanized supply lines that are prone to corrosion failure.

Common Causes of Burst Pipes in the Tri-Cities

Frozen Pipes

While the Tri-Cities don't experience extreme winters, occasional cold snaps can freeze pipes in unheated spaces, exterior walls, garages, and crawl spaces. Frozen water expands and cracks pipes — damage often shows up only after thawing.

Aging Galvanized Pipes

Many homes built before the 1970s still have galvanized steel pipes. These corrode from the inside out and eventually fail, often without warning. We commonly see this in older Coquitlam and Port Moody homes.

Tree Root Intrusion

Mature trees in older Tri-Cities neighbourhoods can send roots through pipe joints, eventually causing breaks. This is especially common in main sewer lines.

Water Pressure Issues

Excessive water pressure (above 80 PSI) stresses pipe joints and fittings. We install pressure regulators to prevent this.

Corrosion and Wear

Even modern copper pipes can develop pinhole leaks from electrolysis or aggressive water chemistry. Soldered joints can fail over time.

Construction Damage

Renovation projects sometimes inadvertently damage pipes. We frequently respond to leaks discovered weeks after construction work.

If a Pipe Bursts: Immediate Steps

1. Turn off the main water valve immediately. It's typically located in the basement, garage, or utility room near where the water line enters the house.
2. Turn off the breaker for affected areas to prevent electrical hazards.
3. Open faucets to drain remaining water in pipes.
4. Move valuables away from water.
5. Call us — average response under 60 minutes.

Burst Pipe Repair Process

1. Emergency Response

We arrive fast and immediately help shut off water if you haven't already. We assess the damage and locate the burst.

2. Diagnosis

For visible bursts, repair planning is straightforward. For hidden pipe failures, we use moisture meters, thermal imaging, and acoustic detection to pinpoint the exact location.

3. Repair Strategy

Depending on the situation, we'll either repair the section, replace it entirely, or recommend a partial re-pipe if the surrounding pipe is also at risk of failure.

4. Quality Restoration

We complete the repair using code-compliant materials and methods. All work is pressure tested before we leave.

5. Recommendations

We help you understand whether other pipes in your home are at similar risk and whether preventive replacement makes sense.
